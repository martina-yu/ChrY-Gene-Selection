# ChrY-Gene-Selection
**Sample:** Carl Zimmer Personal Genome (publicly available)  
**Chromosome:** Y  
**Task:** Part 1A — Top 10 Genes by Mutational Burden  
**Reference Build:** GRCh37 (hs37d5)  
**Tools:** bcftools v1.16 + SnpEff v4.3t (GRCh37.75)  
**Date:** 2026-04-05

## Method

### Input data
- `zimmer_chrY.vcf` with 2,998 germline SNPs on chromosome Y, all passing GATK VQSR quality filtering (VQSR (GATK ApplyRecalibration v2.8), SNP mode, ts_filter_level = 99.0). 
  
### Database

- Genome Reference Panel Database:  `hs37d5 (GRCh37/hg19)`
- Gene Models: `Ensembl Release 75 (GRCh37.75)`. This legacy version is required for 100% compatibility with the GRCh37 reference build.

### Software

- VCF Quality Control: `bcftools (v1.16)`
- Functional Annotation: `SnpEff (v4.3t)`
- `Python == 3.11` with `pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn`

## Analytical Workflow

### Step 1: Quality Control (QC)

The Ti/Tv ratio (1.32) is checked against chrY expectations. Our pipeline identifies heterozygous "artifact" calls (18.2%). Since the Y chromosome is hemizygous in males, these are correctly attributed to mapping errors in highly repetitive regions (e.g., palindromes and X-transposed regions) rather than true variants.

### Step 2: Functional Annotation

Each of the 2,998 variants is processed to determine its location, all variants are classified as strictly genic, upstream/downstream, or intergenic. Noting that only 3 missense variants exist across the entire chromosome, which is consistent with strong purifying selection on the Y chromosome.

### Step 3: Gene Prioritization

Only variants falling strictly within gene bodies (introns, exons, UTRs) are counted toward the burden.

Genes are all ranked based on raw count.

#### Biological Notes on Top Genes

**PCDH11Y** (protocadherin 11 Y-linked, [[UniProt:Q9Y5H8]]): The largest gene on chrY (~742 kb), expressed in the brain and involved in neural cell adhesion. Its high variant count is partly a function of gene length. Notably, it carries 2 MODERATE-impact missense variants (Y:4,968,368 and Y:4,968,655), both homozygous alternate — the only missense variants in the top 10 genes.

**NLGN4Y** (neuroligin 4 Y-linked, [[UniProt:Q8N0W4]]): A synaptic cell adhesion molecule and Y-linked paralog of NLGN4X. All 39 variants are intronic or synonymous, suggesting no predicted functional impact on the protein.

**TTTY14** (testis-specific transcript Y-linked 14): A long intergenic non-coding RNA (lincRNA). All 24 variants are intragenic — located within the gene body but outside any annotated transcript exon. Functional significance is unknown.

**UTY** (ubiquitously transcribed tetratricopeptide repeat Y-linked, [[UniProt:O15550]]): A histone H3K27 demethylase with roles in male fertility and immune function. All 24 variants are intronic or synonymous; no missense variants detected.

**TBL1Y** (transducin beta-like 1 Y-linked): A component of the NCoR/SMRT transcriptional repressor complex. All 21 variants are intronic.

**PRKY** (protein kinase Y-linked): Annotated as a pseudogene in GRCh37.75, though its functional status is debated in the literature. 19 variants, predominantly intronic.

**HDHD1P1, KALP, TPTE2P4**: Pseudogenes with 15–17 variants each. The relatively high variant counts in pseudogenes likely reflect relaxed purifying selection compared to protein-coding genes.

**LINC00278**: A lincRNA with 11 intronic variants. Located in the pseudoautosomal boundary region (PAR1-proximal), which may explain some variant enrichment.
